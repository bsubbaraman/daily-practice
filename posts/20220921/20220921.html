Got a late start to my daily practice today, so it's somewhat abbreviated. In planning a turntable for phenakistoscopes, I'm realizing that this is similar to Tiffany Tseng's Spin/Build In Progress projects for process-oriented documentation. I think I can draw from her designs here, with some tweaks to the electronics to use what we already have in lab. I've started recreating my board from Making It class in SVG-PCB. A main change I'd like to make is using some smaller package--like an H-Bridge--to drive the stepper motor rather than another breakout board; I'm hoping this will clean the board up a bit, and be something a bit different.

reflections & notes

Half the work of electronics is remembering the name of all the footprints/etc that I'm using. I actually think SVG-PCB might make the repurposing of old designs much quicker, because the overhead of opening KiCad is significant for me right now.
